Constitutional monarchy

Government Structure

Panama Nation is governed as a constitutional monarchy. The King oversees the system, the Prime Minister coordinates the ministers, and town representation flows upward through Parliament Members and Mayors.

Hierarchy

How power flows through the nation

King Oversees the Prime Minister and gives final approval on major decisions
Parliament Parliament Members / MEPs are appointed by Mayors and can become Ministers
Mayors Own towns and appoint citizens to Parliament
MEPs Town representatives with access to Minister roles
Citizens Vote, grow towns, and help create stronger representation
Prime Minister Ensures Ministers are doing their jobs and hears suggestions from Mayors and Citizens
Minister of Finance Manages money, budget, grants, and loans
Minister of Public Works Creates jobs, assigns work, and sets pay
Minister of Construction Builds the City Plan and allocates materials
Minister of Foreign Affairs Manages relations with other nations

Representation rule

Parliament seats scale with population

Towns receive 1 Parliament seat per 20 citizens. If a town is too small, it needs to grow to earn a stronger voice in government.

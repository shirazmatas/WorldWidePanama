Constitutional monarchy

Government Structure

Panama Nation is governed as a constitutional monarchy. The King oversees the system, the Prime Minister coordinates the ministers, and town representation flows upward through Parliament Members and Mayors.

Hierarchy

How power flows through the nation

King Final approval on major decisions
Prime Minister Coordinates ministers and escalates citizen concerns
Minister of Finance Budget, grants, and loans
Minister of Public Works Jobs, assignments, and pay
Minister of Construction City plans, plots, and materials
Minister of Foreign Affairs Diplomatic relations with other nations
Parliament Members Appointed by Mayors and eligible for minister roles
Mayor Owns the town and appoints Parliament Members
Citizens Vote, grow towns, and earn Parliament seats

Role 1

King

Head of state

Oversees the Prime Minister, makes sure the nation is improving, and keeps every ministry pointed in the right direction. The King also gives final approval on major decisions.

Role 2

Prime Minister

Head of government

Ensures all Ministers are doing their jobs and makes sure suggestions from Mayors and Citizens are heard and acted on.

Role 3

Minister of Finance

Economy and budget

Manages the nation's money, sets the monthly national budget, and decides which towns receive government grants or loans. Those decisions are then brought to the King for final approval.

Role 4

Minister of Public Works

Labor and jobs

Makes sure there are job opportunities for citizens, assigns work to people, and sets the pay for each job based on the budget.

Role 5

Minister of Construction

City planning

Creates the City Plan, including districts, buildings, and empty plots, and ensures building materials are available and allocated to city projects.

Role 6

Minister of Foreign Affairs

Diplomacy

Handles relationships with other nations and keeps Panama Nation on good terms with everyone.

Role 7

Parliament Members

Town representation

Appointed by their Town Mayor. Parliament Members are the only people eligible to become Ministers.

Role 8

Mayor

Town leadership

Mayors own towns and can appoint their citizens to Parliament, either through a town vote or directly.

Representation rule

Parliament seats scale with population

Towns receive 1 Parliament seat per 20 citizens. If a town is too small, it needs to grow to earn a stronger voice in government.

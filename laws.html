Nation law code

Panama Nation Laws

This page is a template law register written in a statute style. Each law uses § markers and includes the date it was added so the record stays readable and easy to revise.

§ 1.01

Civility and public conduct

Added on July 10, 2026

All residents, allies, and visitors must conduct themselves with basic civility in public channels, on site, and in nation spaces.

  • Harassment, slurs, and targeted abuse are prohibited.
  • Spam, disruption, and griefing of community spaces are prohibited.
  • Disputes must be raised through leadership or moderation channels.

§ 1.02

Territory and claim protection

Added on July 10, 2026

Nation claims, roads, farms, and storage areas are protected assets and may not be altered without permission from the responsible leader or steward.

  • Do not build inside claims without approval.
  • Do not remove blocks, chests, signs, or fixtures from protected infrastructure.
  • Temporary access for work projects must be approved before use.

§ 1.03

Trade standards and pricing

Added on July 9, 2026

Market stalls, export listings, and public offers must be presented honestly and kept current when price or availability changes.

  • Misleading item names, fake stock, and bait pricing are not allowed.
  • Promised delivery times should be respected or updated quickly.
  • Shared market facilities must remain organized and clean.

§ 1.04

Military use and defense readiness

Added on July 8, 2026

Weapons, armor, and defensive stockpiles may be maintained for nation protection, but they must be stored responsibly and used only for legitimate defense or approved ops.

  • Unauthorized removal of gear from defense storage is prohibited.
  • Training areas should remain separate from public storage where possible.
  • Leadership may issue temporary readiness orders during conflict.

§ 1.05

Amendment and interpretation

Added on July 8, 2026

These laws may be revised, expanded, or repealed by nation leadership. If a rule is unclear, the published interpretation from leadership applies until amended.

  • New laws should receive a section number and added date.
  • Superseded laws should be marked as amended or repealed.
  • Interpretations should be recorded in writing when they affect enforcement.
